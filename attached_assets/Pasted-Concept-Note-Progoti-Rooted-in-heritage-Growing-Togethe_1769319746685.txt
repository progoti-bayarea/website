Concept Note – Progoti                   

“Rooted in heritage, Growing Together”
Overview

Progoti is an affiliate program under the Association of Bengalis (AoB), Bay Area, CA, USA. We are a professional network for Bengali-origin early-career professionals* —individuals at the launch or relaunch of their professional journeys. Our aim is to connect members across diverse fields, foster mentorship, and celebrate cultural heritage while enabling career growth and collaboration.


Objectives
Build a Professional Network for Bengali-origin early-career professionals in the Bay Area, with opportunities for collaboration worldwide.


Foster Career Growth & Knowledge Sharing through discussions on career paths, industry insights, and skill-building opportunities.


Promote Cultural Identity & Belonging by celebrating heritage while fostering inclusivity and diversity.


Encourage Mentorship & Peer Support by inviting experienced leaders as panelists and speakers.


Enable Targeted Discussions in domains such as R&D, Technology, Biotech, Finance, Law, unconventional careers, career transition, and entrepreneurship/immigration.


Outcomes
6-Month Outcomes (Short-Term)
Build and grow a WhatsApp community.


Organize ~6 events (every 2–3 months) to establish visibility.


Launch a mailing list to track membership and engagement.


Initiate collaborative events with university groups and partner organizations.


Launch community platforms (Slack/Discord/Circle + initial website) after Event 1.


Begin building a social media presence (LinkedIn, Instagram).


1-Year Outcomes (Annual)
Expand and consolidate a strong membership base with steady mailing list growth.


Ensure consistent participation in events.


Strengthen collaborations with the university-based student and external organizations.


Broaden outreach through Facebook and structured engagement campaigns.


2-Year Outcomes (Long-Term Vision)
Deepen partnerships with professional networks and organizations.


Scale programs/events to gain regional or national recognition.


Position Progoti as a go-to hub for Bengali-origin professionals.


Support the launch of regional chapters in other areas if volunteers emerge.
Team
Core Team: 8 members (expandable up to 10, excluding Advisor). Members are researchers and professionals from STEM fields with event-planning, networking, and leadership experience at Stanford, UC Berkeley, UCSC, UMD College Park, and in organizations such as ISCB, AnitaB.org, AAAS, STEMPeers, SciROI, Hack4Impact, and Google DSC.


Advisor: A senior professional providing strategic guidance. Advisors are typically Bengali-origin unless exceptional circumstances arise.


Role Distribution: Each Core Team member oversees a functional area (Events, Outreach, Partnerships, Communications, Mentorship).
Outreach Plan
Initial Phase: Leverage personal/professional networks, LinkedIn, and Bengali/South Asian student organizations in Bay Area universities.


Partnerships: Collaborate with professional associations, universities, and non-profits in the US, India, and globally.


Visibility: Host in-person events every 2–3 months; maintain an active online presence via WhatsApp (Year 1), expanding digital platforms as the network grows.
Structure & Governance
Steering Committee: Core Team + Advisor, responsible for strategic decisions.


Core Team Responsibilities:


Public Relations, Collaborations, and Expansions


Documentation & Internal Logistics


Social Media & Event Promotions


Finance & Fundraising


Working Groups: Formed for initiatives/projects, led by the Steering Committee, staffed primarily by volunteers.


Election & Membership Renewal
Term: 12 months.


Continuation or Step-Down: At term end, core-team members may continue (with team approval) or step down.


Replacement: Stepping-down members may nominate a successor, subject to Core Team approval.


Eligibility: Preference for volunteers who contributed to Working Groups or with prior leadership experience.


Advisors: Includes current core-team advisor and former core-team members of the Progoti team. Do not count toward the 10-member cap and are not part of the election cycle.


Decision-Making
Consensus-driven as the primary mode.


Fallback: If consensus fails, a two-thirds (2/3) Core Team vote decides.


Escalation: Strategic/unresolved issues may be referred to the Advisory Board or Advisor(s).


Funding (Year 1)
No funding requested from AoB.


Events funded via registration fees (Eventbrite/Meetup) or participant-paid refreshments (coffee meetups).


From Year 2 onwards: seek sponsorships and partnerships for larger events.


Progoti will operate financially independent of AoB’s core budget.


Mutual Benefits – Progoti & AoB
How Progoti Benefits from AoB:
Credibility and visibility through affiliation.


Access to AoB’s professional and cultural network.


Guidance from AoB leadership and alignment with broader initiatives.


How AoB Benefits from Progoti:
Strengthens AoB’s engagement with early-career professionals in the US and globally.


Adds a professional development dimension to AoB’s primarily cultural portfolio.


Broadens AoB’s reach into academic, corporate, and entrepreneurial communities.


Enhances AoB’s long-term sustainability by engaging the next generation of leaders.

*Definition: Early-Career Professionals
For Progoti, this includes Bengali-origin individuals at the launch or relaunch of their careers:
Students & Trainees: University/graduate students, postdocs, or professional trainees.


First Steps into the Workforce: 0–7 years of professional experience.


Career Pivoters: Transitioning into new fields.


Re-entrants: Returning to work after a career break.
